Let

$$
I=\iint\limits_{D} y\,\mathrm{d}x\,\mathrm{d}y,
$$

where $D$ is the closed region enclosed by

$$
\left(\frac{x}{a}+\frac{y}{b}\right)^3=\frac{xy}{c},\quad (a,b,c>0).
$$

We choose a rotation-stretch transformation

$$
\left \{
\begin{array}{c}
    u=\frac{x}{a}+\frac{y}{b} \\
    v=\frac{x}{a}-\frac{y}{b}
\end{array}
\right .
\quad \Leftrightarrow \quad
\left \{
\begin{array}{c}
    x=\frac{a}{2}(u+v) \\
    y=\frac{b}{2}(u-v)
\end{array}
\right .
$$

The integal becomes

$$
I=\iint\limits_{D}y\,\mathrm{d}x\,\mathrm{d}y
=\iint\limits_{D'}\frac{b}{2}(u-v)\left|\frac{\partial (x,y)}{\partial (u,v)}\right|\,\mathrm{d}u\,\mathrm{d}v
=\frac{ab^2}{4}\iint\limits_{D'}(u-v)\,\mathrm{d}u\,\mathrm{d}v.
$$

And the new region $D'$ is enclosed by 

$$
u^3=\frac{ab}{4c}\left(u^2-v^2\right),\quad (a,b,c>0).
$$

The region is symmetric about the $u$-axis, so the integral simplifies to

$$
I=\frac{ab^2}{4}\iint\limits_{D'}u\,\mathrm{d}u\,\mathrm{d}v
=\frac{ab^2}{4}\int_0^{\frac{ab}{4c}}u\,\mathrm{d}u\int_{-\sqrt{u^2-\frac{4c}{ab}u^3}}^{\sqrt{u^2-\frac{4c}{ab}u^3}}\mathrm{d}v
=\frac{ab^2}{2}\int_0^{\frac{ab}{4c}}u^2\sqrt{1-\frac{4c}{ab}u}\,\mathrm{d}u.
$$

Let

$$
t=\sqrt{1-\frac{4c}{ab}u} \quad \Rightarrow \quad u=\frac{ab}{4c}(1-t^2),\quad \mathrm{d}u=-\frac{ab}{2c}t\,\mathrm{d}t.
$$

Finally, the origin integal becomes

$$
\begin{aligned}
I&=\frac{ab^2}{2}\int_1^0\frac{a^2b^2}{16c^2}(1-t^2)^2t\left(-\frac{ab}{2c}\right)t\,\mathrm{d}t=\frac{a^4b^5}{64c^3}\int_0^1(1-t^2)^2t^2\,\mathrm{d}t\\
&=\frac{a^4b^5}{64c^3}\int_0^1\left(t^2-2t^4+t^6\right)\,\mathrm{d}t=\frac{a^4b^5}{64c^3}\left(\frac{1}{3}-2\cdot\frac{1}{5}+\frac{1}{7}\right)=\frac{a^4b^5}{840c^3}.
\end{aligned}
$$
