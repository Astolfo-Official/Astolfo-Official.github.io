Let

$$
I=\int_0^1 \frac{\ln x}{x^2-x-1}\,dx.
$$

Factor

$$
x^2-x-1=(x-\alpha)(x-\beta),\qquad
\alpha=\frac{1+\sqrt5}{2},\;\beta=\frac{1-\sqrt5}{2}=-\frac1\phi,\quad \phi=\frac{1+\sqrt5}{2}.
$$

Then

$$
\frac{1}{x^2-x-1}=\frac1{\sqrt5}\left(\frac{1}{x-\beta}-\frac{1}{x-\alpha}\right),
$$
so

$$
I=\frac1{\sqrt5}(J_\beta-J_\alpha),\qquad
J_c:=\int_0^1 \frac{\ln x}{x-c}\,dx.
$$

---

### Residue part

For

$$
F_c(s)=\int_0^1 \frac{x^s}{x-c}\,dx,
$$
using a keyhole contour for
\(\frac{\log z}{z-c}\) with branch cut on \((0,\infty)\), differentiating at \(s=0\) gives

$$
J_c=\frac{\operatorname{Li}_2(1/c)}{c},
$$
where \(\operatorname{Li}_2\) is the dilogarithm.

Hence

$$
I=\frac1{\sqrt5}\left(\frac{1}{\beta}\operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac1\beta\right)-\frac{1}{\alpha}\operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac1\alpha\right)\right)
=\frac1{\sqrt5}\left(-\phi\,\operatorname{Li}_2(-\phi)-\frac1\phi\operatorname{Li}_2\left(\frac1\phi\right)\right).
$$

Using standard dilogarithm identities and \(\phi^2-\phi-1=0\), this simplifies to

$$
I=\frac{\pi^2}{5\sqrt5}.
$$

Therefore

$$
\boxed{\int_0^1 \frac{\ln x}{x^2-x-1}\,dx=\frac{\pi^2}{5\sqrt5}}.
$$

Numerically,

$$
\frac{\pi^2}{5\sqrt5}\approx 0.8827642541.
$$
